WORK RESUME ABOUT

A brochure for the Georgia Tech Salsa Club designed using Adobe Illustrator and Canva.

Being that I am the Advertisement Executive for the club, it's my responsibility to manage the social media profiles of the Salsa Club and to generate artwork in order to make our presence on campus known loudly and clearly. This was a piece I cooked up in not too long but it is one I can't help but keep looking at. I suppose that might be vain but I am honestly just really proud of how it came out. Something about the color palette is really pleasant and interesting. My mom liked it so I'll consider it a win.

The exterior side is above. The interior is below. I wanted a design that would stand out amongst the inevitable hundreds of brochures that incoming students at Georgia Tech would receive. Something you can spot from a hundred feet away and something that is unequivocally modern and flashy.

My philosophy was to remove unnecessary detail that my predecessor kept in his design and simply everything down to one font and two to three powerful colors (a tasteful red, black, and white). The photos on the left portion are reminiscent of a portion of a balloon arch, which are used in celebrations and events, which the club's social events often feel like.

The following are some of the templates I designed to introduce our new executive board. I as well went for something clean and definitely abstract yet welcoming. The Buzz Gold 80 (Somewhere between Buzz Gold and Buzz Gold 60, official Georgia Tech colors) means the posts do not feel totally cold and industrial but rather are more inviting and warm, especially when paired with the lovely portraits of the executives.

I animated them in order to add some visual flair and fun, and to make the pieces feel more like a true composition, scrapbook-like in how they come together smoothly. The animation also provides a bit of anticipation and piques the interest. I took inspiration from Kanye West's The Life of Pablo album art, which uses repetition across the canvas in a sort of flashy way.
