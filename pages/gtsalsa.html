WORK RESUME ABOUT

In the Spring of 2020 through Summer I served as both the Advertisement and Marketing Executive for the Georgia Tech Salsa club. It was my responsibility to manage the social media profiles of the Salsa Club and to generate digital art pieces like posters, animations, and photography.

I redesigned the brand identity by creating an updated logo and drove up signee numbers even during the pandemic by executing a successful brand refresh during a time of great uncertainty.

I wanted a to manifest a design that would stand out amongst the inevitable hundreds of brochures that students at Georgia Tech get. Something you can spot from a hundred feet away and is clearly GT Salsa. I learned that you can make a design both loud and sophisticated if you have the right constraints. My philosophy, working with the Salsa Club, was to remove unnecessary detail and simply everything down to a couple of colors and to use typography that demonstrates the intensity of the art.

In regards to the deep red (not a usual Georgia Tech color), I collaborated with president Nicole Diaz and we had a really great conversation about the feelings evoked by different colors. Salsa is profoundly associated with the color red so I made the executive call, even though she expressed a really great argument for gold. The compromise we made was that the following few posts would use official Georgia Tech colors like 'Buzz Gold'. Fascinating how passionate people get about color and team identity. Something to keep in mind when trying to communicate the identity of a community through visual design.
