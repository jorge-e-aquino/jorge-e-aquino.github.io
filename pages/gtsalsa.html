Being that I am the Advertisement/Marketing Executive for the Georgia Tech Salsa club, it's my responsibility to manage the social media profiles of the Salsa Club and to generate artwork in order to make our presence on campus made known loudly.

The exterior side is above. The interior is below. I wanted a design that would stand out amongst the inevitable hundreds of brochures that incoming students at Georgia Tech would receive. Something you can spot from a hundred feet away and something that is unequivocally modern and flashy.

My philosophy was to remove unnecessary detail and simply everything down to one font and two to three powerful colors in order to make the information more digestible. I learned that you can make a design both loud and sophisticated if you have the right constraints.

In regards to the deep red (not a usual Georgia Tech color), I collaborated with president Nicole Diaz and we had a really great conversation about color theory and what associations certain colors have with certain ideas. Salsa is associated with the color red so I made the executive call, even though she expressed a really great argument for gold. The compromise I made was that the following few posts would use official Georgia Tech colors. It is really fascinated how passionate people get regarding color. Something to keep in mind.

The following are some of the templates I designed to introduce our new executive board. I as well went for something clean and loud, like the previous artwork. I really learned how animations really play into the perceived quality of a social media post. My teammates expressed specifically that the animation made such a significant difference in how attractive the post was, in their eyes.

I learned how little one actually needs to communicate visually when producing posts designed for the busy digital world of Instagram. When it comes to introducing our members, I could have included each of our favorite spot on campus, favorite quote, major, and etc. but I chose to eliminate all of this information and simply stick to a unified theme that emphasizes seeking the attention of a user instead of trying to communicate as much as possible. The information is actually saved to the Highlights feature of Instagram, which, according to market data, is where users tend to spend more time reading and paying attention to written content. That is why I placed the written portion of the bios in the Highlights section.
