My first foray into working with the Creative Cloud culminated around the futuristic and gaudy style of Baugasm, an art style named after the art movement "Bauhaus."

These pieces were initialized in Illustrator and then I exported them into Photoshop for most of the work. I think some of them are truly beautiful if I had to give myself some praise. The typography was implemented both as a sort of stylish watermark but also to practice complimenting abstract color landscapes with the industriousness of typography.

This next one is one of my favorites. I was really excited to watch The Lighthouse and I decided to make a little poster just for the fun of it. I studied up on typography and wanted to make a poster reminiscent of posters from 60's psych rock bands but constrained to a simple structure. I realized how beautiful human faces look when paired with a complimentary color palette, and being that the film is shot in grayscale, the gradient and vanishing typography really work well together. I really got into the flow of creating posters that I find visually attractive even if they are abstract.

I always base my designs off of real things, like orchid petals, trees against a blue sky, the human body, or celestial bodies. Studying the art of Baugasm helped me learn how to marry maximalism with minimalism in a way that invites conversation, and hopefully engages viewers to want to find out more.
